Welcome to the Robot IAT

You are invited to participate in a research study. The purpose of this study is to measure peopleâ€™s attitudes towards humans and robots. Benefits of doing research are not definite; but we hope to learn that particular groups of people have varying attitudes towards humans and robots. We expect to enroll 200 participants in this study. There are no direct benefits to you in this study activity.

You will complete three tasks: two brief questionnaires and an IAT in which you will sort words and images into categories as quickly as possible. You should be able to complete the tasks in less than 10 minutes total. When you finish, you will be asked some questions related to robots and then receive your results.

(1) completing a brief questionnaire on general information about yourself,

(2) an association test where you will quickly sort words and images into categories, and

(3) a brief questionnaire about robots.

Please note:

  • Your participation in this study is completely voluntary. You may stop at any time. Declining to participate or stopping your participation will not have any negative effects.
  • All responses are anonymous.
  • You must be 18 years or older to participate.
  • The study will take about 10 minutes to complete.
  • No costs are associated nor are payments provided for being in this study.
  • This study is considered to be minimal risk of harm. This means the risks of your participation in the research are similar in type or intensity to what you encounter during your daily activities.

Data exchanged on this site are protected by SSL encryption and no Personally Identifiable Information about you will be collected. IP addresses are routinely recorded using cookies, but are completely confidential. The researchers and the University of Nevada, Reno will treat your identity and the information collected about you with professional standards of confidentiality and protect it to the extent allowed by law. You will not be personally identified in any reports or publications that may result from this study. We will do this by maintaining limited accessibility to the research data on encrypted password-protected systems.
